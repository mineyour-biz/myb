Setting up your MY₿ Phone

To decrease the likelihood of leaking any usable private information or metadata:

  1. DO NOT INSTALL A SIM CARD for initial set up! 
  2. Turn the phone on.
  3. Accept permissions.
  4. Connect your phone to a wifi network away from your home or workplace.
  5. Turn on a VPN before doing anything more.

CalyxVPN is preinstalled for this purpose- simply press the button to activate & your IP address is protected for the remainder of set up.


From the home screen, swipe up then look for this icon in the app drawer.

After you've set up CalyxVPN (or installed custom VPN configurations), feel free to add your SIM card or connect to familiar wifi access points.

Open f-droid to search for application updates & new free Open Source apps you'd like to use.

Some F-Droid apps we strongly recommend:

  • Session Messenger (like Signal- but no phone number required!)
  • Bitwarden (an encrypted password manager)
  • Bromite (like Brave mobile browser- but far more private!)
  • NewPipe (anonymous YouTube viewer app)
  • Samourai Wallet (for CoinJoined Bitcoin [BTC])
  • BlueWallet (for Lightning Payments & watch-only Bitcoin wallets)
  • WallETH (for Ethereum, MATIC, BSC, AVAX, & other EVM chains)
  • Monerujo Wallet (Monero [XMR]) 
  • We'll keep adding to this list over time

If you really need or want to use apps from Google Play - find them in the Aurora Store instead.


(If for some reason apps don't download from within Aurora Store- try uninstalling then reinstalling it from within f-droid)

You are ready to start using the built-in privacy features of your phone!

Staying private

Get rid of old habits

This will mean filling the void with new habits- start by exploring the apps we've listed above.

If you fall into the old habits, or become complacent, it is easy to undo several days (or even weeks) of hard work.

This means that using any unencrypted messaging apps- and especially sending unencrypted email- must be cut out of your regular routines.

Positive peer pressure

Let's face it, your friends influence you a lot.

But you can influence them too.

Talk to them about why increasing your privacy together would be a positive move- similar to how you might all engage in a weight loss or physique change challenge.

Even if they do not agree to take all the same steps you want to, they will at least begin to honor your wish to use privacy-respecting apps like Session (or at least Signal) to communicate with you.

Becoming 100% untraceable

First things first: all of the major carriers are tracking your movement, and logging both your calls and texts... all of them.

On the calls and texts, you have options.

The tracking of your location is only possible to undo with practice.

And even then, all info your phone does leak to the mobile towers is logged by the carrier, who often gives it away to various government & law enforcement agencies w/o due process.

The most practical tool is an anti-tracking Faraday bag, which was included with your MY₿ phone.

If you don't need to use your phone- or want it to disappear from the wireless grid- simply turn it off, and keep it stored in the bag.
